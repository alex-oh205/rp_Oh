Results


Discussion

As indium content (x) increases in 30 nm thick (Bi₁₋ₓInₓ)₂Se₃ films, the Seebeck coefficient increases in magnitude (becomes more negative), showing a strong and statistically significant negative correlation with x. However, this increase is relatively small. At the same time, sheet resistance increases sharply, causing conductivity to decrease significantly. As a result, the thermoelectric power factor (S²σ) decreases with x, also with strong statistical significance.

While the Seebeck coefficient alone improved, the overall thermoelectric performance declined due to reduced conductivity. This disproves the initial hypothesis that adding indium would enhance thermoelectric performance and supports the idea that topological surface states play a beneficial role, aligning with theoretical predictions but contradicting prior results by other studies.
